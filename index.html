Welcome!

This website is designed for families to explore how children learn to speak and listen, why these skills matter, and how adults can create supportive environments that boost language and literacy development from a young age.

Language Development Milestones

Children’s language progresses through predictable stages. Understanding these can help you support them better at every age and stage. From babbling to full conversations, each phase is vital to cognitive and social growth.

Language Development Chart

Age Group Milestones Examples
Infants (0–12 months)
  • Babbling
  • Eye contact
  • Responding to sounds
  • Says “ba-ba” or “da-da”
  • Looks at caregiver’s face during feeding
  • Turns head to voice or music
Toddlers (1–3 years)
  • First words
  • Short sentences
  • Growing vocabulary
  • Says “mama,” “car,” “ball”
  • Uses 2-3 word phrases: “want juice,” “go park”
  • Understands and uses 200–500+ words
Preschoolers (3–5 years)
  • Storytelling
  • Grammar use
  • Asking questions
  • Tells simple stories: “I went to grandma’s…”
  • Uses past/future tense correctly
  • Asks “Why?”, “How?”, “What’s that?” frequently

Importance of Adult:Child Interactions

Language develops best through loving, responsive interactions. Everyday conversations, storytelling, and play build children's communication confidence and vocabulary.

Tips:

  • Get on their level and make eye contact
  • Ask open-ended questions
  • Pause and let them speak

Supporting Multilingual Children

Children who speak more than one language thrive with support in all their languages. Encourage home language use and connect it to English learning.

Strategies include:

  • Read books in both languages
  • Celebrate cultural traditions
  • Use language-rich experiences like cooking or gardening

Home Learning Environments

Language grows in homes filled with books, stories, music, and conversation. Create routines where children feel safe, heard, and curious.

  • Designate a reading corner
  • Label items around the house
  • Include children in daily talk – from shopping to storytelling
